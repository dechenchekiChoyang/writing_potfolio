About

Writing the gap
between thinking
and doing.

I write about the universal quiet of being human — the ideas we carry but never release, the potential we sense but rarely reach. My work lives in that gap.

Every piece is an attempt to name what most people feel but can't quite say. Not answers. Just better questions, shaped into sentences that stay with you longer than they should.

When I'm not writing, I'm staring — at windows, at blank pages, at the distance between who we are and who we meant to become.

How I write

The work behind the words.

Writing isn't inspiration — it's archaeology. You dig, you sit, you stare. Eventually something surfaces that feels true enough to keep.

I

The feeling first

Every piece begins not with a topic but a feeling I can't name yet. I sit with it until the words start to match.

II

Write badly, then honestly

The first draft is always a lie. The second is closer. By the third, something true usually shows up uninvited.

III

Cut everything comfortable

The best edits remove what you're most proud of. What's left is usually the piece that needed to exist.

IV

Release & resist

Letting go of a piece is its own kind of writing. You practice it every time you hit publish.
